Meet Our Board Members

Christi Milroy

Vice President

With over 28 years of experience in Banking, Finance, and Wealth Management, Christi Milroy brings a wealth of financial expertise to her role as Treasurer for Paws 'N Claws for the Cause. Christi is passionate about making a difference in the lives of individuals facing mental or physical health challenges, especially those who have no safe place for their beloved animals while receiving treatment. By aligning her professional expertise with this compassionate mission, Christi is dedicated to helping the organization create a lasting impact in the community.

Katherine Dunbar Morss, RN

Secretary

Katherine is a single mother of 3 amazing adult children. She was a massage therapist for 12 years before becoming a nurse. Katherine worked as a Home Health RN Case Manager for over 12 years with patients ranging in age from 4 years to 103 years old. She has seen the enormous positive impact of animals in her patients' lives. Many of them live alone and struggle with depression and anxiety. Their animals provide a source of companionship and love that would not otherwise be available. In many cases their animal is what makes their life tolerable. Over the years there were many instances when her patients had to go to the hospital and there was nobody to care for their pet. Katherine has taken animals home herself, reached out to church members for support and other caregivers and neighbors who have taken animals into their homes. There have been occasions when there was no solution and a patient who really needed support refused care because they had no one to care for their pet. An organization that would provide an option for patients to get the care they need and to be secure in the knowledge that their animal is well taken care of would fill a huge need in the community.

JoAnn Lagace

Director

JoAnn Lagace is a dedicated advocate, mentor, and survivor with a passion for helping individuals reclaim their lives while keeping the bond with their beloved pets intact. A retired real estate broker, JoAnn’s journey has been shaped by resilience, personal growth, and an unwavering commitment to others. With over 16 years of sobriety, she brings firsthand experience and deep empathy to those struggling with medical challenges, mental health issues, and substance use disorders. As a trauma-informed and trauma-trained professional, JoAnn understands the complexities of healing, especially for those fleeing domestic violence. Having walked a similar path, she is committed to ensuring that no one has to choose between their safety and their pets. Her mission is to provide support, resources, and hope—helping individuals find stability while preserving the healing power of the human-animal connection. Through her work, JoAnn stands as a beacon of strength, proving that recovery, safety, and second chances are always possible.

Lisa Jacoby

President

Lisa brings a highly diversified skillset developed in both public and private sector leadership positions. Lisa is uniquely qualified, providing over 15 years of management expertise to special districts and municipalities in Colorado, following more than 20 years of experience in land development and construction administration, she has served as a board member on numerous home-owner associations and local governmental entities. Lisa is currently employed with McGeady Becher Cortese Williams, P.C. as Director of District Administration, managing projects, and statutory/constitutional requirements of special districts.
